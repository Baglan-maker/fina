About Kazakhstan

The nature of Kazakhstan is striking in its diversity and splendor. Towering mountain ranges, vast flat steppes, green valleys, sprawling deserts, deep canyons, and turquoise lakes – Kazakhstan has everything to stun even the most experienced traveler. Nowadays, when there are fewer and fewer areas of pristine nature in the world, Kazakhstan boasts an abundance of natural beauty. From time immemorial, our ancestral nomads lived in connection with nature, treating it with care and ensuring sustainability.

The steppes of Kazakhstan

The steppes of Kazakhstan occupy a significant part of the country's territory and represent vast plains with arid climate and grassy vegetation. These steppes serve as an important ecosystem for wildlife and were traditionally used by nomadic peoples for grazing.

Big Almaty Lake

The Big Almaty Lake is a high—altitude lake located at an altitude of about 2511 meters in the mountains of the Trans-Ili Alatau, near Almaty, Kazakhstan. It is known for its turquoise waters and is surrounded by picturesque mountains, making it a popular destination for tourists and nature lovers.

Charyn Canyon

Located in Kazakhstan, it stretches along the Charyn River for 154 kilometers and is part of the Charyn National Park. It is known for its unique rock formations, reminiscent of Monument Valley in the United States, and attracts tourists with scenic views and natural attractions.

Another thing Kazakhstan is proud of is its cultural diversity. People of many nationalities call Kazakhstan their home, having created a unique cultural mosaic here. At the same time Kazakhs cherish their nomadic past, unique traditions, and customs, and pass them from generation to generation.

Kazakh Language

Kazakh is the official language of Kazakhstan and belongs to the Turkic group of languages.

Independence

Kazakhstan declared its independence from the Soviet Union on December 16, 1991, becoming the last of the Soviet republics to declare sovereignty.

Kazakhstan in world politics

Kazakhstan is actively involved in international politics, being an important economic and energy center of Central Asia.

Frequently Asked Questions

Kazakhstan has a continental climate with cold winters and hot summers. The best time to visit is spring (April-June) or fall (September-October).

Citizens of many countries, including Russia, CIS, EU, and the USA, don’t need a visa for short-term visits (up to 30 days).
